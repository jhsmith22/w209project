Gun Ownership and Gun Policy: A Map View

We display gun ownership rates and gun law permissiveness scores on a map view. RAND estimated gun ownership rates in each state with a structural equation model for 2016. The estimates represents the "proportion of adult, noninstitutionalized residents in each U.S. state and year (1980â€“2016) who live in a household with a firearm."

We aggregate the gun laws for each state into an aggregate "Gun Policy Permissiveness Score" using the law classifications from the RAND gun law database. RAND assesses each law as either "permissive" or "restrictive." We assign each permissive law with 2 points, each restrictive law with -2 points, and assign 1 point if no law exists in that category. RAND breaks out each law class for its application to handguns and long guns, and we assign points accordingly. For example, if a state had restrictive background check laws for handguns and for long guns, that totals -4 points (-2 for handguns and -2 for long guns). We also apply our own weights from 1 to 5 to the different law classes, to represent the estimated relative importance of each law class in affecting gun use. We assign registration a 5, as the most impactful law class. We assign carrying a concealed weapon (ccw), Castle Doctrine, and permit to purchase a 3. We assign firearm sales restrictions a 2. We assign background checks, open carry, safety training, and local laws preempted a 1, as the least impactful law classes.

Correlation with Gun Ownership Rate

These charts display a state's estimated Gun Ownership Rates (RAND data) on the x-axis and measures of Firearm Death Rates (CDC data). Metric 1 is firearm death rate per capita. Metric 2 is the share of deaths in the state that are due to firearms. Both metrics show a general (while noisy) upward-sloping trend, with states that have some of the higher gun ownership rates also having some of the highest firearm deaths.


Select areas of the chart to compare states between Metric 1 and Metric 2.
