The Big Picture

The Big Picture looks at the role of firearms in fatalities nationwide. What share of deaths are attributed to firearms? What share of suicides? Homicides? Unintentional injuries? This data places the more granular dives into the firearm violence data within the broader conversation about fatalities in the United States.

Role of Firearms in Deaths by Age Group: 1999 - 2020

This chart shows the share of deaths in each age group that are due to Firearms for all years 1999 - 2020 (CDC data). The share of deaths from firearms is highest in the 15-24 age group, at 26%. 2020, the first year of COVID, has the highest rate for this age group in the last 20 years.

The orange cirlces represent 2020.

The blue circles represent prior years: 1999 - 2019.

Use the slider to see the trend in different years.

The role of guns in suicides, homicides, and unintentional injuries

This chart shows the mechanism used for suicides, homicides, and unintentional injury deaths. In 2020, firearms were used in about half of suicides, about 80% of homicides, and less than 1% of unintentional injury deaths.
